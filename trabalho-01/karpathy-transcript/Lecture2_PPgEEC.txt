Assignment 1
LLM Deep Dive (Reverse Classroom)

===== PAGE 11 =====
Overview
In this assignment, you will study Large Language Models (LLMs) through a reverse classroom 
approach.
Instead of attending a lecture on the topic, you will:
●
Study a comprehensive Deep Dive lecture (by Andrej Karpathy)
●
Reconstruct the concepts in your own way
●
Teach them as if you were the instructor
The goal is not to reproduce the content, but to demonstrate that you can understand, structure, 
and communicate complex ideas independently.
This assignment also includes a peer interaction component, designed to promote reﬂection and 
intellectual exchange in a natural and unscripted way.

===== PAGE 12 =====
Learning Objectives
By completing this assignment, you should be able to:
●
Explain the end-to-end pipeline of a modern LLM
●
Describe the role of tokenization, embeddings, and Transformers
●
Understand the training paradigm (next-token prediction)
●
Identify limitations and open challenges in LLMs
●
Communicate technical ideas clearly at a graduate level
●
Critically engage with another person’s explanation 

===== PAGE 13 =====
Part I - Your Lecture (Individual Work)
You will create your own explanation of the Deep Dive content.
Format
●
Platform: Loom (www.loom.com)
●
Maximum: 10 minutes per video
●
Expected: 3 videos (≈ 25–30 minutes total) 

===== PAGE 14 =====
Suggested Structure (ﬂexible, not rigid)
You are encouraged, but not forced, to organize your videos around:
1. The Big Picture
Explain the overall pipeline:
●
Tokens → Embeddings → Transformer → Output
●
What the model is actually doing
●
Focus on clarity and conceptual flow.
2. Technical Deep Dive
Explain core technical mechanisms in more detail:
●
Attention mechanism (intuition + formal perspective)
●
Transformer blocks (self-attention, feed-forward layers)
●
Training process (objective, data, scaling)
●
Inference behavior 
●
Students are encouraged to include diagrams, equations, or 
examples where appropriate
3. Critical Reﬂection
Provide a critical and analytical perspective:
●
What are the limitations of LLMs?
●
What aspects were unclear or under 
explained in the source material?
●
What are common misconceptions?
●
What questions remain open?
●
This section should demonstrate 
independent thinking, not just 
reproduction of content.

===== PAGE 15 =====
Part II - Peer Interaction (organic, asynchronous)
You will watch the videos produced by your partner (from the ﬁrst class activity).
Your role is simple:
●
Engage with your colleague’s explanation in a thoughtful and honest way.

===== PAGE 16 =====
Part II - How to do it
Use the comment feature in Loom.
There is no rigid template, but your interaction should naturally include:
●
What you found clear
●
What you did not fully understand
●
What you think is missing
●
At least one meaningful question
After that:
●
The author is expected to respond
●
A short back-and-forth may emerge
Important Principles
●
This is not a checklist exercise
●
This is not scripted interaction
●
Do not coordinate responses in 
advance
●
Do not treat this as a formality
The goal is to create a genuine technical 
exchange

===== PAGE 17 =====
Academic Integrity
You may use AI tools to:
●
organize your ideas
●
improve clarity
However:
●
The explanation must reﬂect your own understanding
●
You must be able to defend everything you present
●
Blind copying or passive generation is not acceptable
